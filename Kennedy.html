NASA Kennedy Space Center

August 2019 - Present

The Oscar project is a trash to gas system that will improve the effectiveness of long duration missions such as gateway and the ISS. Some of the waste from these missions will be salvaged to create useful resources such as rocket fuel or water. A key milestone for Oscar is to be tested in a microgravity environment to verify the effectiveness of the system. This verification is slated to happen this holiday season aboard a sub orbital rocket achieving a couple minutes of micro gravity. These few minutes of microgravity will give valuable information that would ordinarily not be obtainable using conventional methods such a drop tower or a parabolic flight. Nicholas is assisting the OSCAR team in achieving this goal by helping with preparations for this test. This includes hardware assembly and CAD work.
