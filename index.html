Physics and Computer Science

Nicholas Bartel is an undergraduate at Carthage pursuing a degree in Physics with a strong background in Computer Science. Within his first week at Carthage, Nicholas joined a team working to develop a small earth-imaging satellite to monitor global forest health. He joined the subsystem team that was integrating the Attitude Determination and Control System, ADACS, and played a pivotal role in ensuring the success of the satellite through design and develop of a magnetic testing apparatus to validate the ADACS. Nicholas assisted in developing technology for gauging fuel volume in microgravity environments. During his involvement he designed and built the electronics system for a Blue Origin suborbital flight research payload employing this technology. Over the summer of 2019, Nicholas was selected to participate in a summer research opportunity at the NASA Langley Research Center. During this position he designed a low speed wind tunnel model of the Hercules Martian lander and predicted its aerodynamic coefficients. Currently Nicholas is an intern at NASA Kennedy Space Center. He is working with a team to develop a technology that will reclaim resources from waste such as fuel and water.
